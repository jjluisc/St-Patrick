St. Patrick's

We`re proud to offer a wide range of programs designed to enhance your skills and prepare you for your academic and professional goals. From pre-ICFES and pre-university courses to English language programs, this institution provides a welcoming learning environment and a team of highly qualified teachers to support you in achieving your objectives.

Mission

The mission of the SAN PATRICIO INSTITUTE is to prepare young people and workers from Ocaña with a solid foundation for employment in the business, technology, sales and services, education, industrial technology, and humanities (especially languages) sectors. By offering regular training programs and continuing education, the Institute develops the relevant academic and labor competencies in its students through innovative and participatory methodologies, where they are the protagonists of their own learning. In this way, the Institute contributes to the integral development of the Colombian population and the region through the articulation of businesses with the changing world of work.

Vision

The SAN PATRICIO INSTITUTE is a leading Ocaña-based institution dedicated to providing high-quality, job-relevant training. By fostering a culture of critical thinking and innovation, we empower our students to become positive agents of change in the region. Rooted in the Salesian tradition, the Institute promotes research as a fundamental tool for learning and development.

Values

-Effectiveness in applying methodologies and tools in the face of competition within national policies.
- Regional relevance by harmonizing regular and continuing education activities and research with the needs of the communities in the institution's area of influence.
- Maintain employment and progress in employment, carrying out transactions between jobs and roles within the same organization to meet new job or career development requirements.
- Work-based training, with values that allow learners to develop individually and socially.

General objective

Lifelong, professional, personal, social, and cultural learning, grounded in a holistic conception of the person and organized within the Institutional Educational Project, structured in flexible curricula that are not subject to the level and grade systems typical of formal education
